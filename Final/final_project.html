After seeing the above graphs, I wanted to explore the variables that contribute the most to the difference between the winners (winPlacePerc == 1) and the worst losers (winPlacePerc == 0). To do this I fit a Random Forest Classifier to a dataset for each solo and duo games with binary labels (winPlacePerc is either 1 or 0). I then got the values for feature importance, the larger the values, the greater the importance.

For solo games, the top 5 features of greatest importance to winPlacePerc are killPlace, longestKill, killStreaks, walkDistance, and boosts. For duo games, the top 5 features of greatest importance to winPlacePerc are boosts, walkDistance, killPlace, weaponsAquired, and longestKill. While these shed some light on what differentiates a good player from a bad player, it does not show the difference between a good player and an average player since winPlacePerc is continuous it is difficult to assign labels for the Random Forest Classifier to work. I believe there are other methods to explore feature importance but I did not have enough time to explore them.

With what I have so far, I believe that for solo games, skill based features such as killPlace (Ranking in match of number of enemy players killed.), longestKill (Longest distance between player and player killed at time of death. This may be misleading, as downing a player and driving away may lead to a large longestKill stat.), killStreaks (Max number of enemy players killed in a short amount of time.), and boosts (Number of boost items used.) are the most important in separating good players from bad players. This is more apparent in solo games because you are in a one versus all situation where your raw skill has to prevail to win the overall match.

For duo games however, it appears that looting (picking up items and weapons) contributes heavily to the difference between winning and dying early. Also, skill based features like killPlace, and longestKill are still important.
